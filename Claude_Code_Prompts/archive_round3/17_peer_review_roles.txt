## Step-by-step
1. Create a class `XReviewer(Reviewer)` with a `BiasProfile` declared inline.
2. Implement `review(conclusion, context) -> ReviewReport`. The review method is itself a registered method (`@register_method(name=f"review_{role}", version="1.0.0", method_type=JUDGMENT, owner="founder", status="active", nondeterministic=True)`).
3. Call `reviewers.register(XReviewer)` at module-bottom so importing populates the registry.
4. Append a row to `docs/peer_review/reviewers.md` with columns: | Role | Prior | Known blindspots | Tools |.
5. Write at least 3 unit tests per role against fixture conclusions: one that should pass cleanly, one with a `minor` finding, one with a `blocker` finding.

ROLE SPECIFICATIONS (prior, tools, representative finding categories)
methodological.py
  prior: "Was the right method, at a defensible version, applied to the right input?"
  tools: `methods.REGISTRY`, method calibration reports.
  categories: wrong_method, unversioned_method, version_regression, deprecated_method.

evidential.py
  prior: "Does each cited chunk actually support what the conclusion claims?"
  tools: retrieval + nli_scorer.
  categories: citation_mismatch, cherry_picking, load_bearing_on_weak_chunk.

statistical.py
  prior: "Is the uncertainty honestly quantified? Is sample size sufficient?"
  tools: calibration data, evaluation metrics.
  categories: underquantified_uncertainty, insufficient_n, miscalibrated_discount.

adv_literature.py
  prior: "Does the external literature contain counter-evidence not engaged with?"
  tools: literature.external_claim_match, external_battery data.
  categories: unaddressed_counter_evidence, outdated_reference.

replication.py
  prior: "Can I re-derive this conclusion from the stated inputs alone?"
  tools: method registry invocation, cascade traversal.
  categories: non_replicable, hidden_input, non_determinism_without_seed.

rhetorical.py
  prior: "Is the argument rhetorically clean or does it smuggle moves past the reader?"
  tools: LLM.
  categories: motte_and_bailey, equivocation, loaded_term, hidden_normative.

humility.py
  prior: "Are unresolved points and open questions faithfully surfaced?"
  tools: LLM + corpus retrieval.
  categories: overclaim, suppressed_unresolved, premature_consensus.
