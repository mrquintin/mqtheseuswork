\begin{tldr}
Currents is the firm's live commentary surface. It pulls public events
in from X, decides whether the firm's corpus has anything substantive
to say, and either writes a citation-grounded opinion or abstains.
Every published opinion respects a set of structural invariants that
prevent it from speaking outside the corpus or revealing private
material. This guide explains the pipeline, the invariants, and how
to read an opinion's audit trail.
\end{tldr}

\section{What Currents is}

Currents is a separate origin from the main Codex. The public page is
\route{/currents}; the founder-facing controls live inside the Codex
under \route{/(authed)/founder-currents}. Each ``Current'' is one
\texttt{EventOpinion} row written by the firm in response to one
outside event.

\screenshot{04_currents_public_feed.png}{The public \route{/currents} feed, with two opinions visible and one event in abstention.}{cu-feed}

\section{The pipeline}

The pipeline is documented authoritatively in
\file{docs/currents/PIPELINE.md} and operator-side in
\file{docs/operator/CURRENTS.md}. Summary, top to bottom:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0.2em]
  \item \textbf{Discovery.} The X recent-search endpoint is asked for
        recent high-engagement posts (and founder-curated accounts as
        a side channel). Configured search queries are available as
        targeted augmentation, not as the default seed.
  \item \textbf{Significance floor.} Posts below the significance and
        raw engagement floors are dropped.
  \item \textbf{Persistence + dedupe.} A surviving post becomes a
        \texttt{CurrentEvent}. Duplicates of an existing event are
        suppressed.
  \item \textbf{Enrichment.} Embeddings, near-duplicate matching,
        topic enrichment.
  \item \textbf{KB relevance gate.} Event embeddings are compared
        against the corpus. If fewer than three relevant conclusions
        are found, the event abstains explicitly --- it does not
        become an opinion.
  \item \textbf{Opinion generation.} The generator retrieves up to
        twelve firm conclusions and requires at least three to clear
        the relevance threshold. The LLM is required to cite each one
        inline as \texttt{[C:<id>]}.
  \item \textbf{Publication.} \route{/v1/currents} on the FastAPI
        backend returns the resulting \texttt{EventOpinion}; the
        Next.js \route{/api/currents} route proxies it onto the
        public \route{/currents} page.
\end{enumerate}

\section{The eight invariants}

A published Currents opinion must satisfy all eight of the following
structural invariants. They are enforced by the generator and the
publish path; an opinion that fails any one of them is rejected before
it reaches the public feed.

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0.3em]
  \item \textbf{Grounded.} Every non-trivial claim in the opinion text
        is backed by an inline \texttt{[C:<id>]} citation to a firm
        Conclusion or Principle. Free-floating assertions are not
        allowed.
  \item \textbf{Source-respecting.} Citations resolve only to
        publicly-safe corpus surfaces. Private transcripts and
        documents may inform internal reasoning but must not appear
        as public citation targets.
  \item \textbf{Quorum.} At least three relevance-cleared firm
        conclusions back the opinion. If the corpus cannot supply
        three, the system abstains rather than under-cite.
  \item \textbf{Voice-consistent.} The opinion is written in the firm's
        first-person voice rather than recapping or paraphrasing the
        source post. The article surface is \texttt{EventOpinion},
        not raw \texttt{CurrentEvent}.
  \item \textbf{Event-anchored.} Each opinion is bound to exactly one
        \texttt{CurrentEvent}. Multi-event commentary lives in the
        articles pipeline, not in Currents.
  \item \textbf{Revocable.} Every opinion is publishable iff
        \texttt{revokedAt IS NULL}. Operator revocation is the
        canonical inverse of publication; there is no separate
        \texttt{isPublic} flag.
  \item \textbf{Abstention-honest.} When the relevance gate fails,
        the system records an \texttt{abstentionReason} on the event
        and surfaces ``the firm does not yet have enough material to
        comment.'' Abstentions are a public stance.
  \item \textbf{Provenance-complete.} Each opinion ships an audit
        trail: the source post, the relevance scores, the conclusions
        cited, and the generator parameters. The audit trail is
        addressable from the public page.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Reading the audit trail}

Open any opinion at \route{/currents/<id>}. Below the body you will
find:

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0.2em]
  \item \textbf{Source post.} The original X post the opinion responds
        to. Always present, always external.
  \item \textbf{Cited conclusions.} A list of \texttt{[C:<id>]}
        references, each click-through to the firm's Conclusion on
        the public site.
  \item \textbf{Relevance scores.} The score each cited conclusion
        had against the source event at generation time.
  \item \textbf{Generator metadata.} Model identifier, prompt version,
        retrieval window, timestamp.
  \item \textbf{Revocation banner} (if revoked). The opinion is hidden
        from the feed but its row and audit trail remain readable for
        accountability.
\end{itemize}

\section{Operator surfaces}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0.2em]
  \item \page{OperatorPulse} card on the founder dashboard --- green
        when the token and at least one ingestion path are configured,
        a scheduler cycle has reported, and the last 24 hours contain
        both events and opinions. Red lists the precise missing link.
  \item \route{/(authed)/founder-currents} --- per-opinion operator
        controls: revoke, hold for review, adjust event-level metadata.
  \item \file{noosphere/scripts/diagnose\_currents\_pipeline.py} ---
        non-writing diagnostic. Prints booleans, counts, and errors
        only; never prints the bearer token. Run this before blaming
        the UI.
  \item \env{CURRENTS\_X\_INGESTION\_DISABLED=true} --- kill switch.
        See Guide 6.
\end{itemize}

\section{Common failure modes}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*,itemsep=0.3em]
  \item \textbf{No opinions in 24 hours.} Likely either an X token
        problem or a corpus-relevance problem. Check
        \page{OperatorPulse} first; it disambiguates.
  \item \textbf{Opinions look like they are recapping rather than
        opining.} Check that you have at least a few firm conclusions
        in the topic area. The generator falls back to thinner prose
        when its conclusion retrieval comes back near-empty.
  \item \textbf{An opinion cites a private document.} This is a
        violation of invariant 2 and should not happen. Revoke the
        opinion immediately and file a critique --- see Guide 6.
\end{itemize}
