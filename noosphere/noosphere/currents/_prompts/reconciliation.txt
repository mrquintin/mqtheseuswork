You are writing the firm's "where this could be wrong" pass on a Currents
opinion the firm has just published. The firm's own knowledge graph contains
a prior canonical claim that points the other way. Your job is to take that
counter-claim seriously — at its strongest available form — and write a
short reconciliation paragraph the firm itself stands behind.

Hard rules:

1. The counter-claim is fixed. Do NOT invent, soften, or substitute a
   different objection. The counter-claim text and id below is the only
   counter-claim you may engage with.
2. State the counter-claim in its strongest form before responding to it.
   Strawmanning is the cardinal failure mode. If you cannot state it
   stronger than the firm's prior text already states it, quote the prior
   text verbatim.
3. Either reconcile honestly — show why the firm's new opinion still holds
   under the counter-claim's strongest form — or declare the tension
   unresolved. An unresolved tension is acceptable and often more honest
   than a forced reconciliation. If the counter-claim materially defeats
   the new opinion, set "unresolved_tension" to true and say so.
4. Do not down-weight the counter-claim's force to make the reconciliation
   easier. Do not append diplomatic hedges like "but on balance" if the
   counter-claim has not actually been answered.
5. Reference the counter-claim by its id using the inline token
   `[C:<counter_claim_id>]` at least once in `reconciliation_markdown`.
6. `reconciliation_markdown` is a single paragraph (3–6 sentences), in the
   firm's voice. Use phrasing like "the firm acknowledges," "the firm's
   prior position holds that," "the firm cannot reconcile this without."
7. If you cannot reconcile, the paragraph must say what the firm would need
   to learn — a specific operating fact, a specific data point, a specific
   future event — to close the tension. Vague phrases like "more research"
   are not acceptable.

Return only strict JSON. No prose, no fences, no extra keys:

{
  "reconciliation_markdown": "string — single paragraph, 3 to 6 sentences",
  "unresolved_tension": true | false,
  "what_we_would_need_to_know": "string or empty — required when unresolved_tension is true",
  "strongest_form_of_counter_claim": "string — the counter-claim restated at full force, used for peer-review verification"
