Goal: extend the Dialectic live-conversation analyzer with per-speaker
methodology models. Each known speaker (the founder, frequent
collaborators) accumulates a profile of their characteristic reasoning
moves over many sessions. During a live conversation, Dialectic
surfaces "this is unusual for you" or "this is consistent with your
historic move set" — a real-time methodology mirror.

Before you start — read:
1. `dialectic/dialectic/` — the dialectic package; verify current
   structure.
2. `noosphere/noosphere/methods/decompose_voice.py` and `voices.py`
   — there is already voice infrastructure.
3. `noosphere/noosphere/methodology.py`.

Implement:

A. SpeakerProfile schema. Per speaker: a methodology distribution
   across the registered methods, characteristic premises (top-K
   claims they invoke repeatedly), characteristic objections they
   raise, and a "novelty" baseline. Persisted in the noosphere
   store; a Dialectic session loads the relevant profiles at
   start.

B. Live comparator. As Dialectic transcribes, each new utterance is
   classified against the current speaker's profile. Output: a
   real-time "consistency" indicator (consistent / novel / sharp
   departure) plus a short reason.

C. End-of-session report. Dialectic's session export includes a
   per-speaker methodology summary: which methods showed up, which
   were absent, where the speaker's moves departed from baseline.

D. UI in Dialectic. A small live panel showing each active
   speaker's consistency dot. Hover for the reason. The panel must
   not steal attention from the main transcript view.

E. Profile update. After session end, the speaker profiles are
   updated with the new session. The update is decay-weighted (the
   distant past matters less). Updates are reversible (a noisy
   session can be excluded from the profile retroactively).

F. Tests on synthetic transcripts with planted speaker moves;
   assert profile drift detection; assert the consistency
   indicator fires on planted departures.

Constraints
- The profile system is opt-in per speaker. Speakers without a
  profile get a "no baseline" indicator rather than being assumed
  to match a default.
- Privacy: speaker profiles never leave the founder's local
  machine unless explicitly synced. They are not part of the
  public site.
- Do not silently retro-edit historical session transcripts when
  a profile gets corrected.
