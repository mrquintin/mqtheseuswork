Goal: a recurring self-critique pass over the firm's published corpus.
Once a quarter, every published article is re-read by a fresh
adversarial swarm with the explicit task: "find what the firm got
wrong, what aged poorly, and what is now embarrassing." The swarm's
findings either: (a) trigger a revision, (b) attach as a "later view"
addendum on the article, or (c) get explicitly dismissed with reason.

Before you start — read:
1. The peer-review swarm (prompt 21).
2. The revision engine (prompt 16).
3. The publication signing work (prompt 30).

Implement:

A. Self-critique scheduler. A quarterly job (existing scheduler)
   selects every published article whose latest review is older
   than 90 days, queues each for a self-critique run.

B. Self-critique reviewer. A new reviewer in
   `noosphere/noosphere/peer_review/self_critique.py` that
   takes (article, original_evidence_at_publish_time,
   evidence_now). The reviewer's prompt is explicit: identify
   claims that have aged poorly, citations whose source has
   changed, predictions that resolved against the firm, and
   blind spots that have become visible.

C. Verdict shape. The reviewer outputs structured findings:
   {claim, was_supported_by, now_supported_by, verdict ∈
   {still holds, weakened, contradicted by new evidence, no
   longer supported}, recommended_action ∈ {revise, addend,
   dismiss}}.

D. Founder triage. Findings land in the unified attention
   queue (prompt 34) at high severity. The founder picks
   action per finding.

E. Public addendum. When the action is `addend`, the article
   page renders a dated addendum block: "On <date> the firm
   re-reviewed this article and found <summary>. See the
   appended note." The addendum is not a stealth rewrite —
   the original text remains unchanged; the addendum is
   visibly later content.

F. Tests. Synthetic article + synthetic later evidence;
   assert findings shape; assert the addendum lifecycle.

Constraints
- The self-critique uses fresh evidence the firm gathered after
  publication. It does NOT use evidence available at publish
  time and pretend that was an unfair surprise.
- Original article text is immutable through this path. Only
  the revision engine (prompt 16) edits original text; this
  prompt produces addenda.
- The self-critique swarm uses a different reviewer
  configuration from the original publication's swarm to
  reduce shared blind spots.
