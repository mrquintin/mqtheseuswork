Goal: improve counter-claim retrieval quality in the Currents
dialectic engine (Round 17 prompt 27). The first version retrieves
opposing claims by embedding similarity + the contradiction-direction
probe. In practice this surfaces false positives — opposing-sounding
claims that on inspection do not actually contradict the firm's
opinion. Tighten the matching.

Implement:

A. Hybrid retrieval. Combine three signals:
   (1) embedding similarity above threshold
   (2) NLI judgment of "actually contradicts" rather than just
       "opposes in tone"
   (3) cascade-graph evidence: the candidate must be backed by
       at least one source the firm has previously taken
       seriously.
   All three must hold for a candidate to surface.

B. Strawman detector. After the reconciliation is generated, an
   automated check confirms the counter-claim text in the
   reconciliation matches the retrieved counter-claim's actual
   text. Paraphrase that softens the counter is flagged as a
   strawman and forces regeneration.

C. Sample-based audit. Instrument 20 random recent
   reconciliations and rate them on a rubric (faithful
   counter / strawman / fabrication / no-meaningful-counter).
   Audit results land in
   `docs/research/internal/Currents_Dialectic_Audit_<stamp>.md`.

D. Threshold calibration. Use the audit results to calibrate
   the retrieval thresholds. Document the chosen thresholds and
   their justification.

E. Reconciliation tone discipline. The reconciliation prompt is
   updated to require the strongest available form of the
   counter-claim. Diplomatic softening is the failure mode.

F. Tests. Synthetic Currents opinions with planted counters;
   assert hybrid retrieval surfaces them; assert strawman
   detector catches paraphrased softening; assert the no-counter
   honest-note path fires when no counter clears all three
   thresholds.

Constraints
- Do not lower the bar to surface more counter-claims. False
  positives erode trust faster than false negatives.
- Threshold values live in the unified config from prompt 11,
  not as inline magic numbers.
- The audit is repeatable; the rubric used in this audit
  becomes the rubric for future audits.
