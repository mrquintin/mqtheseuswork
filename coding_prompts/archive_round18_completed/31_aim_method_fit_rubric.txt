Goal: formalize Aim-Method Fit — the third of the five working
criteria (THE_META_METHOD §2.3). Of the five criteria,
Aim-Method Fit is the weakest in the firm's current implementation:
the MQS rubric (Round 17 prompt 01) treats it as a soft LLM
judgment without a precise rubric. Sharpen it.

Before you start — read:
1. `THE_META_METHOD.md` §2.3 — the criterion's definition.
2. `noosphere/noosphere/inquiry/mqs.py` (post prompt 05's
   reorganization).
3. `docs/methods/MQS_Specification.md` from Round 17 prompt 01.

Implement:

A. Sharper definition. Aim-Method Fit asks: does this method
   actually answer THIS question? A valuation method gives
   valuations; if the question is about product strategy, a
   valuation method has poor fit. The rubric must operationalize
   "actually answer" without collapsing into Domain Sensitivity.

B. Rubric. Define a 5-level rubric:
   - 0: method's outputs cannot answer the question type
   - 1: outputs answer a related but different question
   - 2: outputs answer part of the question
   - 3: outputs answer the question with caveats the method
        cannot articulate
   - 4: outputs answer the question with explicit caveats
        within the method's competence
   Each level has at least two worked examples in the spec.

C. Question typology. Build a typology of question types the
   firm encounters: descriptive, predictive, normative,
   strategic, methodological, classificatory. Each method
   declares which question types it serves.

D. Mismatch detection. The MQS scorer computes Aim-Method Fit
   from: (a) the question type inferred from the conclusion's
   target, (b) the question types the producing method
   serves, (c) the worked-example match. Mismatches surface as
   low Aim-Method Fit and feed into the gating composite.

E. Backfill. Re-score every existing conclusion's
   Aim-Method Fit. Conclusions that drop a tier on the
   composite go to the founder's queue.

F. Tests. Worked examples from the rubric exercise the scorer
   deterministically; question-type inference is tested
   against a small labeled set.

Constraints
- Do not retire methods based on this rubric alone. A method
  with low Aim-Method Fit on some questions may still be the
  best available method for the questions it does fit.
- The question-type taxonomy lives in code, not just prose,
  and is shared with the question classifier from prompt 29.
