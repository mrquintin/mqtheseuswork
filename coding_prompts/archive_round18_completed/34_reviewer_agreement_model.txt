Goal: build a predictive model of reviewer agreement. The red-team
tournament (prompt 16 above) collects when reviewers agree and
disagree on objection severity. Use that data to predict, for a new
conclusion, whether the swarm will likely converge — and surface
that prediction at review time so the founder knows when to expect
contention.

Before you start — read:
1. The tournament results from prompt 16.
2. `noosphere/noosphere/peer_review/swarm.py`.
3. `noosphere/noosphere/peer_review/severity.py`.

Implement:

A. Feature extraction. From the tournament corpus, extract
   features per (conclusion, swarm-config, objection): topic
   embedding, claim type, source mix, methodology used,
   originating swarm config, severity, reviewer-id (provider).

B. Agreement model. Train a simple model predicting
   inter-reviewer agreement (a continuous score) from the
   conclusion's features. Use a held-out tournament shard for
   evaluation.

C. Pre-review prediction. At new-conclusion review time, the
   model predicts expected agreement; the prediction surfaces
   on the review page as a "expected contention" pill.

D. Use case: reviewer routing. When predicted agreement is
   low, the swarm rotates additional reviewers (more
   diversity, more cost). When predicted agreement is high,
   the swarm runs a smaller config (cost discipline). The
   policy is documented and ablation-testable.

E. Founder dashboard. A "reviewer agreement trends" widget on
   the dashboard tracks the model's calibration over time —
   does it actually predict agreement, or is it just adding
   noise?

F. Tests. Synthetic tournament data with planted agreement
   structure; assert the model recovers the structure;
   assert routing policy honors the predicted agreement.

Constraints
- The model is a predictive aid, not a gate. A predicted
  high-agreement conclusion still gets the full swarm if
  the founder requests it.
- Cost savings from routing must be reported alongside any
  potential loss in coverage. The firm should not silently
  prefer cheap swarms.
- The model itself is subject to drift; track its calibration
  and surface drift via the existing drift detector.
