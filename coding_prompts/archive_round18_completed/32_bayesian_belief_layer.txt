Goal: a Bayesian-network layer over the cascade graph. The cascade
weights conclusions by source credibility × edge weight, but the
algebra is ad-hoc. A proper Bayesian network gives the firm
principled marginal-probability calculations on conclusion truth
given evidence updates, with explicit conditional dependencies.

Before you start — read:
1. `noosphere/noosphere/cascade/graph.py` and `traverse.py`.
2. The revision engine from Round 17 prompt 16.
3. The source-credibility ledger from Round 17 prompt 19.

Implement:

A. Bayesian DAG construction. From the cascade graph, derive a
   directed acyclic graph (BN-DAG) of binary truth-valued
   nodes for each Claim. Edges encode "if parent claims hold,
   what is P(child claim holds)?". Conditional probability
   tables (CPTs) are seeded from cascade weights and source
   credibilities, then refined.

B. Inference engine. Use a small in-process BN library
   (pgmpy or hand-rolled junction-tree inference for graphs
   under N=200 nodes; for larger graphs, importance sampling).
   Output: marginal probability per claim, with credible
   interval reflecting CPT uncertainty.

C. Update on evidence. When evidence arrives (a source
   retracts, a forecast resolves, a peer-review verdict
   lands), the BN's prior is updated and marginals
   recomputed. Compare the recomputed marginal to the
   firm's stored confidence; deltas feed the revision engine.

D. CPT learning. Where the firm has enough resolved cases
   for an edge type, the CPT is fit to data rather than
   stipulated. Sparse CPTs use Laplace smoothing.

E. Founder UI. A "Bayesian view" tab on each conclusion
   shows the marginal, the most-influential parent claims,
   and the sensitivity of the marginal to each parent
   (i.e. if parent X were retracted, marginal would drop
   from p to p').

F. Tests. Synthetic 5-node BN with known CPTs; assert
   inference exactness; assert update propagates correctly;
   assert sensitivity analysis matches analytic ground truth.

Constraints
- The BN does not replace the cascade. The cascade remains
  the primary representation; the BN is a derived view used
  for principled inference.
- Marginal probabilities are not displayed publicly without
  founder review; this is a founder-side tool first.
- For graphs above the practical inference limit, the UI
  shows "approximate inference (n=K samples, CI=[a,b])"
  rather than implying exactness.
