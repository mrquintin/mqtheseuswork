Goal: a formal method-retirement workflow. Round 17 added drift
detection and failure-mode catalogs, but no clean way to retire a
method that has earned retirement. Without a workflow, retired
methods linger as zombies in the registry.

Before you start — read:
1. `noosphere/noosphere/methods/_registry.py` and the composition
   work from Round 17 prompt 05.
2. The drift detector (Round 17 prompt 04).
3. The Householder ablation result from prompt 15 above —
   this prompt is the workflow that lets the firm act on it.

Implement:

A. Retirement criteria. A method qualifies for retirement
   review if any of: (1) sustained drift alert > 60 days,
   (2) ablation reveals zero contribution beyond a baseline,
   (3) zero invocations in 90 days, (4) every conclusion
   produced by it has been revised away. Document the
   criteria in `docs/methods/Method_Retirement_Criteria.md`.

B. Retirement state machine. A method has states
   {ACTIVE, UNDER_REVIEW, DEPRECATED, RETIRED}. Transitions
   are explicit; the registry refuses calls to RETIRED methods
   (raising a typed error pointing to the replacement) and
   warns on DEPRECATED.

C. Founder review path. A method entering UNDER_REVIEW
   produces a memo template at
   `docs/methods/retirement/<method>.md`: rationale,
   conclusions affected, replacement method, migration plan,
   sunset timeline. Founder accepts or rejects.

D. Migration. When a method moves to DEPRECATED, every
   conclusion it produced is flagged with a sunset banner;
   reanalysis with the replacement method is scheduled.

E. UI. The methods page shows retirement state. Public
   methodology shows DEPRECATED and RETIRED methods with
   tombstone styling — readers should be able to see what
   the firm has stopped trusting.

F. Tests. Retirement state-machine transitions; refuse-call
   on RETIRED; banner on DEPRECATED conclusions; migration
   triggers reanalysis.

Constraints
- A method cannot move directly from ACTIVE to RETIRED. The
  UNDER_REVIEW step is mandatory and produces a permanent
  record.
- Retired methods remain importable for historical
  re-analysis; calls just refuse with a clear error. Source
  is not deleted.
- Public-side, retired methods do not vanish. They are part
  of the firm's record.
