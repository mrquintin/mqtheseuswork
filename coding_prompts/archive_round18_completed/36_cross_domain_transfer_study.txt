Goal: empirically study cross-domain method transfer. Round 17 added
a `transfer/` package; populate it with a real experiment. Question:
when method M works in domain D, does it transfer to D'? The answer
is rarely "fully" or "not at all"; the firm should measure.

Before you start — read:
1. `noosphere/noosphere/transfer/` package.
2. The domain-applicability bounds work (Round 17 prompt 06).
3. The methods registry — which methods declare which domains.

Implement:

A. Transfer experiment design. Pick 3 method/domain pairs where
   the method has a strong track record (n >= 20 resolutions in
   D). For each, construct a held-out evaluation set in a
   neighboring domain D' the method does NOT explicitly claim.

B. Transfer evaluation. Run the method on the D' set. Measure
   accuracy / Brier / calibration. Compare to (i) the method's
   D performance and (ii) a domain-naive baseline trained on
   D'.

C. Statistical test. For each pair, test whether transfer
   performance is significantly worse than in-domain
   performance. Effect size + CI alongside p-values.

D. Outcome. The experiment has three possible outcomes per
   pair: clean transfer, partial transfer, no transfer. The
   firm publishes whichever it finds; partial-transfer cases
   are most interesting because they illuminate boundary
   conditions.

E. Public surface. A `methodology/transfer-study/page.tsx`
   reports the findings. Each pair's result is presented with
   the same honesty discipline as the QH benchmark — losses
   are not hidden.

F. PDF. `docs/research/Cross_Domain_Transfer_Study.pdf` and
   .tex. Numbers from code; abstract states the headline.

Constraints
- The experiment must NOT modify a method's declared domain
  bounds based on transfer results in this prompt. That's a
  founder decision following the published evidence.
- Held-out sets are frozen at experiment start; do not
  re-curate to chase a result.
- Sample sizes must be honest; n=5 transfer doesn't get a
  conclusion, it gets a "preliminary, n=5" note.
