Goal: an explicit invitation channel for outside experts to critique
specific firm conclusions, with a structured response form, a
moderation flow, and a bounty/credit mechanism that materially
incentivizes high-quality submissions. The firm's methodological
edge depends on inviting the strongest possible external critique;
hoping critique arrives is not enough.

Before you start — read:
1. The PublicResponse model and email pipeline.
2. The response triage work (prompt 31).
3. The signed authorship work (prompt 30) — published critiques
   should be signed similarly when they are accepted.

Implement:

A. Open-critique surfaces. Each public article gets a
   "challenge this" affordance with a structured form: which
   specific claim, what counter-evidence, what method they
   used to derive the counter-evidence, citations.

B. Submission triage. Submissions land in a moderation queue
   distinct from general PublicResponses (prompt 31). The
   queue requires structured judgment: accept (publish + credit
   the critic), partial (private discussion), reject (with
   reason).

C. Credit and bounty mechanism. Accepted critiques publish
   alongside the article with the critic's name and chosen URL.
   For severe critiques (per the severity rubric, prompt 22)
   that meaningfully change the conclusion, the firm pays a
   bounty (configurable, default starts at $500) via the
   firm's existing payouts pipeline OR an explicit "donate to
   charity X" choice the critic selects.

D. Founder UX. Accepting an accepted critique optionally
   triggers the revision engine (prompt 16) and updates the
   article via an addendum (prompt 43). The critic's name and
   contribution are tracked in the lineage.

E. Public listing. A `/critiques` page lists all accepted
   critiques as a hall-of-fame, with the critic's bio (where
   provided) and the article they affected.

F. Tests. Synthetic submission flow; assert moderation states;
   assert credit attribution survives revision; assert the
   bounty payout path is gated by founder confirmation only.

Constraints
- No payout fires without founder confirmation. The system can
  prepare and queue payments; it cannot send them.
- Bounty exists to incentivize substance, not volume. The
  rubric for "severe critique" is published on the /critiques
  page so submitters know what counts.
- A critic's contribution remains visible even if a later
  revision moves the firm's position elsewhere. The lineage
  is honest about what changed why.
