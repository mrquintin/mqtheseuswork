Goal: a single founder dashboard that surfaces "what needs your
attention right now" across every founder-side queue (drift events,
peer-review escalations, source-triage, response triage, open
questions, citation verdicts, retraction propagations, calibration
breaches). The current dashboard is fragmented across many tabs;
priority items get lost.

Before you start — read:
1. `theseus-codex/src/app/(authed)/dashboard/page.tsx` and the
   `DashboardHearth.tsx` component family.
2. The queues introduced in earlier prompts in this batch: 04
   (drift), 16 (revision impact), 18 (source triage), 20
   (citation verdicts), 22 (severity-weighted objections), 31
   (response triage).

Implement:

A. Unified queue API. `GET /api/founder/attention` returns a
   ranked list of items across every queue, with: queue name,
   item id, severity (low/medium/high), age, preview, link.

B. Ranking. Severity > age > queue identity. Within severity,
   age weights up: a high-severity item that has sat for 7 days
   ranks above a fresh high-severity item.

C. Dashboard redesign. The dashboard's primary surface becomes
   the unified attention list. Existing widgets (Hearth, Pulse)
   move to a secondary row.

D. Snooze and dismiss. Each item supports snooze-until and
   dismiss with reason. Snoozed items return at the chosen time.
   Dismissed items are recorded; a high rate of dismissals on a
   given queue is itself a signal that the queue needs tuning.

E. Notifications. A daily morning email summary (using the
   existing email pipeline) lists open high-severity items. The
   email link deeplinks to the dashboard.

F. Tests on a synthetic queue mix; assert ranking, snooze
   behavior, dismiss audit log.

Constraints
- The dashboard does NOT auto-resolve anything. It surfaces; the
  founder decides.
- Snooze cannot be longer than 14 days; longer = dismissal with
  reason "deferred indefinitely".
- The email summary is opt-in per founder.
