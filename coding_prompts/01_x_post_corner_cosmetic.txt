What's actually happening:
- platform.twitter.com renders the iframe with its own border-radius, but the
  area OUTSIDE the iframe's rounded clip path is the wrapper element's
  background. When the wrapper's background is unset (transparent), the user's
  OS-default white shows through at the four corners.
- A safe, dependency-free fix is to: (a) clip the wrapper itself with
  `border-radius` matching the iframe's radius, (b) paint the wrapper with the
  same background token as the surrounding card so any sub-pixel anti-alias
  fringe blends in, and (c) `overflow: hidden` so nothing renders past the
  clip. The Twitter widget renders one direct iframe child whose own rounded
  corners match — clipping the wrapper at the same or slightly larger radius
  hides the leaks without touching widget internals.
