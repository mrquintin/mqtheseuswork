Goal: turn Currents from a one-shot opinion generator into a small
dialectic engine. Each Currents opinion gets at least one canonical
counter-claim drawn from the firm's own knowledge graph plus a
worked-out reconciliation (or an explicit "we cannot reconcile —
here is what we'd need to know"). This makes the firm's public
opinions argumentatively rigorous on first contact rather than
reactively defensive.

Before you start — read:
1. `noosphere/noosphere/currents/opinion_generator.py`,
   `relevance.py`, `enrich.py`.
2. `theseus-codex/src/app/currents/[slug]/page.tsx`.
3. The geometric blindspot work (prompt 24).
4. The severity-weighted objections work (prompt 22).

Implement:

A. Counter-claim retrieval. For each newly generated EventOpinion,
   surface the K nearest opposing claims in the firm's own graph
   (geometric — use the contradiction direction probe). Filter to
   non-revoked, non-private claims that the firm itself has
   endorsed at some point.

B. Reconciliation generator. The opinion generator gets a follow-up
   pass that takes (opinion, top counter-claim) and writes a
   "where this could be wrong" paragraph: either a reconciliation
   or an explicit unresolved-tension note. The pass uses the
   firm's strongest-objection prompt template, not a generic
   "consider the other side" template — the goal is severity, not
   diplomacy.

C. Display. The public Currents page surfaces the reconciliation
   as a collapsible section under the opinion. By default open if
   the unresolved-tension flag is set, closed otherwise.

D. Skip rule. If no counter-claim above a similarity threshold
   exists in the firm's graph, the opinion is published with an
   explicit "no canonical counter-claim found in firm history"
   note, so the absence is honest rather than hidden.

E. Audit. Every reconciliation records which counter-claim it
   referenced, plus the cascade weight at the time. Later
   revisions of the counter-claim should propagate (revision
   engine, prompt 16) into the reconciliation.

F. Tests. Synthetic graph with a planted opposing claim; assert
   reconciliation references it; assert the no-counter case
   prints the honest note rather than fabricating a strawman.

Constraints
- The reconciliation must NOT generate counter-claims out of thin
  air. Every counter-claim must resolve to an existing firm
  Conclusion or a cited public source.
- Strawmanning is the cardinal failure mode. The generator's
  prompt must require the strongest available form of the
  counter-claim, and the peer-review swarm should reject
  reconciliations that down-weight the counter-claim's force.
