## The problem

The Theseus Codex has two distinct review systems with different vocabularies:
1. **Coherence review** (`/q/review`): human-in-the-loop judgment on whether two claims cohere, contradict, or are unresolved. Verdicts: `cohere` / `contradict` / `unresolved`.
2. **Peer review** (`/peer-review/[id]`, tab on conclusion detail): automated reviewer swarm assessment of a conclusion. Verdicts: `endorse` / `challenge` / `abstain`.

These are fundamentally different processes serving different purposes, but neither system explains what it is, how it differs from the other, or what its verdicts mean. A user encountering both for the first time has no way to understand the distinction.

## Your task

Add clear contextual explanations to both review interfaces that explain what the system does, what the verdicts mean, and how it differs from the other review system.
