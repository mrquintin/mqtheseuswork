Goal: a public follow-mechanism that lets an outside researcher track
specific topics, methods, or methodology criteria. When the firm
publishes new material in the followed area — including revisions and
retractions — the follower receives a digest. This is what turns the
public site from a brochure into an active subscription.

Before you start — read:
1. The publication pipeline at
   `theseus-codex/src/lib/publicationService.ts`.
2. The lineage and revision work (prompts 16, 17).
3. Existing email pipeline in
   `theseus-codex/src/lib/responsesEmail.ts`.

Implement:

A. Subscription model. `Subscriber` row with: email, opt-in
   timestamp, scope (one of: methodology[name], domain[tag],
   conclusion[id], firm-wide), unsubscribe token, last-sent
   timestamp. Double opt-in via emailed confirmation link.

B. Subscription form. Public surface; lives on every method
   page (/methodology/[method]), every domain page, every
   article (the article's domain), and the homepage as
   firm-wide. Form posts to
   `POST /api/public/subscribe`.

C. Digest builder. A scheduled job (existing scheduler)
   computes per-scope updates since each subscriber's
   last-sent timestamp: new publications, revisions
   (prompt 16), retraction propagations (prompt 18),
   significant calibration breaches (prompt 12). The
   builder emits a single digest per subscriber.

D. Digest content. Plain text + HTML; each item has a one-
   line summary and a stable URL. The digest is honest about
   negative news: revisions and retractions are not
   downplayed.

E. Unsubscribe. The token in every email maps to a
   one-click unsubscribe. The unsubscribe is recorded with
   reason (free-text optional) for the firm's signal.

F. Tests. Synthetic subscribers + canned events; assert
   correct content per scope; assert double opt-in flow;
   assert unsubscribe returns within one digest cycle.

Constraints
- No tracking pixels in digests.
- No third-party email vendor with telemetry the firm cannot
  audit. Use the existing transport (Resend / SMTP). Receipts
  go through the same path.
- Default delivery cadence: weekly. Subscribers can opt for
  immediate (firm-wide major events only) or monthly.
