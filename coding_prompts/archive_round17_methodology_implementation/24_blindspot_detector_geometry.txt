Goal: a blindspot detector that uses contradiction geometry rather than
keyword heuristics. The current blindspot reviewer
(`noosphere/noosphere/peer_review/blindspot.py`) is largely prompt-
driven. Use the firm's actual unique capability — embedding-space
contradiction detection — to surface neighboring claims the
conclusion's argument fails to engage.

Before you start — read:
1. `noosphere/noosphere/peer_review/blindspot.py`.
2. `noosphere/noosphere/methods/contradiction_geometry.py` and
   `contradiction_probe.py`.
3. The Quintin Hypothesis benchmark (prompt 08) for the geometric
   primitives.
4. `noosphere/noosphere/coherence/locality.py` for neighborhood
   construction.

Implement:

A. Geometric blindspot algorithm in
   `noosphere/noosphere/peer_review/geometric_blindspot.py`:
   1. Build the embedding-space neighborhood of the conclusion's
      core claim within radius r (param).
   2. Filter to claims that the conclusion does NOT cite as
      supports / contradicts / refines / instantiates.
   3. Run the contradiction-direction probe on each. Surface as
      candidate blindspots: claims that score high on the
      contradiction direction but were not engaged.
   4. Rank by combined contradiction score × cascade weight of
      the source the unengaged claim itself rests on.

B. Plug into the swarm as `geometric_blindspot` reviewer with
   severity scoring (prompt 22) wired the same way the existing
   blindspot reviewer is. The two coexist; their union is the
   reviewer's output.

C. Visualization. Founder workspace adds a "geometric blindspots"
   tab to the conclusion review page showing, on the existing
   embedding explorer canvas, the conclusion + its known supports
   + the unengaged neighborhood. Click → drill into the unengaged
   claim's source.

D. Severity wiring. The cascade weight × contradiction score
   product feeds the severity rubric (prompt 22). High-product
   blindspots are high-severity by construction.

E. Tests. Construct a synthetic embedding space with planted
   blindspots; assert the detector recovers them; assert it does
   NOT flag the conclusion's actual citations as blindspots
   (regression: easy false-positive direction).

F. Public-side surface — minimal. The public methodology page for
   `geometric_blindspot` describes the method and links to the
   QH benchmark page (prompt 08).

Constraints
- The detector reads from the same embeddings the rest of
  noosphere uses. No new model.
- Computational budget: a single blindspot run for one conclusion
  must complete in under 5s warm cache and under 30s cold.
- Do not silently merge the two blindspot reviewers' outputs into
  one bag of objections. Each carries its provenance.
