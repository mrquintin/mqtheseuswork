Goal: a first-class "open questions" engine. The firm's transcripts and
conversations contain dozens of unresolved questions that simply fall
out of view — they get mentioned, no one answers them, and the next
topic begins. Surface them, track them, prioritize them, and make
them a public artifact of the firm's intellectual humility.

Before you start — read:
1. `theseus-codex/src/app/(authed)/open-questions/page.tsx` and the
   companion `OpenQuestionPortal.tsx` component (these exist; verify
   their current scope).
2. `noosphere/noosphere/methods/suggest_research.py`.
3. `theseus-codex/prisma/schema.prisma` for `OpenQuestion`.

Implement:

A. Question extractor. `noosphere/noosphere/methods/extract_open_questions.py`:
   given a transcript or document, surface questions that satisfy
   ALL of: (1) interrogative form OR phrasing like "I don't know
   whether", (2) not answered within K turns of the same speaker,
   (3) not redundant with an existing OpenQuestion (paraphrase-
   match against the live registry).

B. Prioritization. Each question gets a priority score from:
   - centrality (does it affect existing firm conclusions?)
   - replayability (would resolving it require expensive new
     work, or is it a literature lookup?)
   - calibration relevance (does it bear on a domain where the
     firm's track record is thin?)
   The score is recomputed on a schedule.

C. Linkage. Open questions can be linked to conclusions whose
   confidence depends on them. When the question gets resolved,
   the linked conclusions go through the revision engine
   (prompt 16) automatically.

D. UI upgrade. The founder workspace open-questions page becomes
   a triage queue with priority sort, filter by domain, and a
   "promote to research" action that writes a `ResearchSuggestion`.

E. Public surface. `theseus-codex/src/app/methodology/open-questions/page.tsx`
   shows public-visible open questions with their dates, a
   "candidate methods to address" list, and (where relevant) the
   linked conclusion-ids that are gated on resolution.

F. Tests on synthetic transcripts; assert the extractor catches
   the planted questions and rejects rhetorical questions that
   the speaker immediately answers.

Constraints
- A question that has already been resolved must not re-surface.
  The resolution event is a write-once row.
- Priority scoring is bounded; do not let centrality dominate
  every other input — a niche but answerable question can still
  rank high if its expected value of information is high.
- Public-visible open questions render the firm's questions, not
  the firm's gossip. The visibility filter is strict.
