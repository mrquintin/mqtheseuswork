Goal: a principle-distillation pipeline that runs across the firm's
entire conclusion corpus and surfaces stable cross-domain principles
— the latent organizing claims the firm keeps re-deriving without
naming. The output is a curated principles index visible to founders
and (where flagged) to the public, with each principle backed by the
conclusions that instantiated it.

Before you start — read:
1. `noosphere/noosphere/ontology.py` — there is already a principle
   distillation primitive; extend it rather than reinvent.
2. `noosphere/noosphere/cascade/graph.py`.
3. `noosphere/noosphere/methods/synthesize_conclusion.py`.

Implement:

A. Cluster principles. Run hierarchical clustering on the
   embedding-space neighborhood of all firm conclusions. Use a
   conservative cluster threshold; the firm prefers fewer, more
   defensible principles over many narrow ones.

B. Principle generator. For each cluster, generate a candidate
   principle (single-sentence claim) using the firm's existing
   LLM client, with the cluster's conclusions as the only
   context. Each principle's draft text must directly cite the
   conclusions it generalizes — no free invention.

C. Founder triage. The principles land in
   `(authed)/principles/queue/page.tsx` as drafts. Founder
   accepts (with edits), rejects (with reason), or merges with
   an existing principle.

D. Principle store. Accepted principles are
   `Principle` rows with: text, generalized-conclusions list,
   creation timestamp, conviction score (computed from the
   number and centrality of underlying conclusions).

E. Public surface. `/methodology/principles` lists public
   principles with conviction-weighted ordering. Each links to
   its underlying conclusions. The page treats principles as
   reviewable artifacts, not slogans.

F. Re-derivation. Principles are not eternal. A scheduled job
   re-runs distillation; if a principle's underlying cluster
   has shifted (new conclusions, retractions), the principle
   surfaces in the founder queue for re-validation.

Constraints
- Principles are not inferred axioms; they are summaries of the
  firm's working positions. The text must read as a position the
  firm is willing to defend.
- The conviction score is conservative: a single high-centrality
  conclusion does not produce conviction; convergence across
  domains does.
- Public principles must declare their domain. The firm avoids
  publishing universal-sounding principles whose underlying
  evidence is domain-narrow.
