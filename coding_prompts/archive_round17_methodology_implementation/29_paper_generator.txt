Goal: an automated paper-generator that turns a coherent cluster of
firm conclusions, methodology, and outcomes into a draft research
paper PDF — abstract, introduction, methods, results, discussion,
limits, references. The point is not to publish the draft as-is; it
is to make it cheap for the firm to convert what it already knows
into outward research output.

Before you start — read:
1. `noosphere/noosphere/docgen/` — current docgen package.
2. `scripts/build_robustness_ledger_pdf.sh` — the existing pdflatex
   build pattern.
3. The lineage and methodology surfaces from prompts 07 + 17.
4. `docs/Theseus_Codex_User_Guide.tex` for the existing TeX style.

Implement:

A. Cluster selector. `noosphere/noosphere/docgen/paper_clustering.py`
   chooses a candidate cluster of conclusions: a connected
   subgraph in the cascade, with a shared methodology root, and
   at least one resolved forecast that touches the cluster.

B. Paper template. A `paper_template.tex.jinja` with sections
   that pull from the firm's existing artifacts: abstract from
   the cluster's distilled principle, methods from the
   methodology profiles, results from the resolved-forecast
   numbers, discussion from the peer-review swarm output, limits
   from the failure-mode catalog (prompt 03), references from
   the citation set.

C. Generator. `noosphere/noosphere/docgen/paper_generator.py`
   takes a cluster id, fills the template, and emits a `.tex` +
   `.pdf` pair under `docs/research/auto/<slug>/`. The generator
   never invents numbers; every number in the paper resolves to
   a row in the database. Numbers without a row generate a
   visible TODO marker rather than fabrication.

D. Review queue. The founder workspace gets a "papers" review
   tab where drafts can be triaged: edit-and-keep, edit-and-
   publish, reject. Edits land in the .tex file via the founder
   workspace's existing text-editing primitives.

E. Public surface. Published auto-papers live under
   `theseus-codex/src/app/research/[slug]/page.tsx` with the PDF
   embedded and a plain-prose summary. Each paper carries a
   "machine-drafted, founder-reviewed" disclosure label.

F. Tests. Synthetic cluster → generator → assert no-fabrication
   property, assert all numerical claims resolve to row IDs,
   assert pdflatex compiles without errors.

Constraints
- The generator does NOT auto-publish. The publication step is
  always human-confirmed.
- The .tex file is the source of truth. PDF is a build artifact;
  do not edit the PDF directly.
- The disclosure label is non-removable. A "founder-reviewed"
  paper still says machine-drafted in the byline.
