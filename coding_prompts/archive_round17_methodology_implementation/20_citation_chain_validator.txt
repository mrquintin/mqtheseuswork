Goal: a citation-chain validator. When the firm publishes "X supports
Y, see source S," the validator confirms that source S actually
supports X — at the textual level, not at the "we cited it" level.
This catches the most common credibility failure mode: paraphrasing
drift, where a chain of citations slowly turns the original source
into something its author would not endorse.

Before you start — read:
1. `theseus-codex/src/lib/oracleCitations.ts`.
2. `noosphere/noosphere/methods/external_claim_match.py`.
3. The source-standing and credibility work from prompts 18–19.

Implement:

A. Validator in
   `noosphere/noosphere/literature/citation_chain.py`. For each
   `OpinionCitation` / `ForecastCitation` / `ConclusionSource`,
   extract: (a) the firm's stated claim about the source, (b) the
   underlying source text excerpt, (c) the relation type (supports /
   contradicts / qualifies / mentions). Run an NLI-style judge with
   the existing `noosphere/noosphere/methods/nli_scorer.py` and
   record an entailment verdict per citation.

B. Verdict schema. Each verdict carries: relation_holds ∈
   {entails, contradicts, neutral, ambiguous}, confidence ∈ [0,1],
   excerpt_used, model_version. Persist as
   `CitationVerdict` rows.

C. Failure handling. When a verdict is `contradicts` or `neutral`
   on a `supports` citation, surface in the founder triage queue
   (extend prompt 18's queue). The article cannot be published
   until every supports-citation has at least an `entails` or
   `ambiguous` verdict, or an explicit founder override with a
   reason.

D. Public surface. CitationPopover shows the verdict pill (green
   for entails, red for contradicts, gray for ambiguous). The
   public reader sees that the firm checks its own citations.

E. Recompute trigger. When a source's standing changes (prompt 18)
   or the source text is updated (a journal correction), every
   citation against that source is re-validated.

F. Tests on synthetic citation pairs with known entailment.
   Mock the NLI judge for deterministic CI runs.

Constraints
- The validator must not call the LLM judge on the entire source —
  use the cited excerpt + a window of context. A verdict written
  using a 200-word excerpt must record exactly that excerpt.
- A `mentions` relation is the weakest possible: do not promote it
  to `supports` even if the LLM judge claims entailment.
- An `ambiguous` verdict is a finding, not a failure. Do not
  escalate it unless the citation is load-bearing (cascade weight
  above a threshold) — then it goes to triage.
